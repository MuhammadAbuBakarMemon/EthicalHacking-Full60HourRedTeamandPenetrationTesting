CMOS (Complementary Metal oxide Semi Conductor) Switch 
this design principle lies at the heart of almost every modern day logic circuit from PC's CPU to the microcontrollers in a smart watch

DESIGN 
we have 2 
1)PMOS (P channel MOSFET) - conducts when the input is low - attached to the top part of the CMOS design - source connected to logic high/binary one 
2)NMOS (N channel MOSFET) - conducts when the input in high - attached to the bottom part of the CMOS design - source connected to logic low/binary zero
connected together both of these are paired together, 
both the PMOS and NMOS's gate terminals are tied together and used as the input to the CMOS Inverter 
both of the drain terminals are tied together and used as the output to the inverter to the CMOS Inverter 

This symmetrical design is what allows 1 transistor to be on whilst the other is off 
THIS MUTUAL EXCLUSIVITY OS WHAT MAKES THE CMOS SO POWER EFFICIENT AND RESPONSIVE 

-gates act as inputs to our transistor 
-drain acts as an output to our transistor 

-Input of 5V to the Gate -> NMOS active -> 0V on the Drain Output 
-Input of 0V to the Gate -> PMOS active -> 5V on the Drain Output
