Computers are constructed from carefully organized groups of logic gates called logic circuits.

This allows the computer to process digital information, as they form the base of the CPU, memory units and other devices.

Logic Circuits are of 2 types 
-Combinational Logic Circuit 
-Sequential Logic Circuit

COMBINATIONAL:
they operate on only the current input provided to them, at the specific moment
no memory or dependency on what happened before, hence the system forgets any previous inputs 
EXAMPLE:
TV remote control ->press channel number 5 -> it changes channel to it no past memory required/utilized (regardless of the channel on is on the switch happens independently of that information)

SEQUENTIAL:
the output depends on both the current input and the past inputs, circuit has some form of memory to remember the previous states
EXAMPLE:
TV remote control -> press the channel up or down button the TV needs to know the current channel to decide which channel to switch to 

ACTIVE HIGH SIGNAL 
HIGH Voltage - 1 - 5V/3.33V
LOW Voltage - 0 - 0V

ACTIVE LOW SIGNAL 
LOW Voltage - 1 - 5/3.33V
HIGH Voltage - 0 - 0V

In real design work or datasheets we will always encounter these voltage levels, hence knowing them helps you communicate with hardware engineers and read technical documents
