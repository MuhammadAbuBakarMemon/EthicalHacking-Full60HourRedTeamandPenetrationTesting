To accurately represent the -ve result in unsigned arithmetic (subtraction) the computer uses something called a carry flag to recognize and handle such situations. And since we are performing unsigned arithmetic here we can not use negative numbers with unsigned here 

Carry flag gives us the signal whether the subtraction operation has needed a borrowing from beyond the available bits. 

Carry flag set to 1 indicates that the borrowing was necessary, suggesting the calculated result isn't fully accurate because of the bit limitation here

Carry flag reset to 0 indicates that no borrow operation was required in the arithmetic, as the operation did not exceed the bit limitations

Assembly Language (that operates underneath the hood) has specific instructions for addition and subtraction that updates the relevant flags accordingly 

Subtraction Syntax 
SUB dest s1 s2 
basically means subtract s2 from s1 and then store the result in the destination, and update the flags accordingly
