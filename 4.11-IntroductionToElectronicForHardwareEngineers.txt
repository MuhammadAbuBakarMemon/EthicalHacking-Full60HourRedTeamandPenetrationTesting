Transistors act as tiny electronic switches, controlling the flow of electrical signals.

What is Current?
Current refers to the movement of electrical charge which is measured in units called Coulombs 

Voltage/Potential Difference 
refers to the difference in electrical energy per unit charge between 2 points in an electrical circuit 
Voltage in lee-man terms is like the pressure that pushing the electrical current through the circuit 

A computer at its lowest level is made from a combination of different electronic components.
1- Power Sources: provide the electrical power needed to run the circuits 
2- Passive Components: these affect current flow and voltage levels, but they can not be controlled or changed by other electronic components e.g resistors and capacitors 
3- Active Components: these are components that can switch between different status like on off or other configurations under the influence of other electronic signals 
Transistors are the most important active components 
4- Conductors: these are materials that connect all the components together (usually wires), and allow electrical signals to flow 

Electronic devices are designed to operate reliably with in a certain range of voltages 

Components inside the computer circuit are regularly switch between voltage levels, (0V and 5V)
each switch takes a tiny amount of time, and this time limits how fast a circuit can complete operations 
If we go for faster switching this requires more electrical power this may increase the speed but also this will increase the heat and can damage our components. This time-dependent behavior of electrical components is a critical design consideration for hardware engineers
