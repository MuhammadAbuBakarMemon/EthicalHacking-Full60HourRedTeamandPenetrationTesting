Computers are billions of two-state (binary) switches managed by the CPU. Every operation sour computer performs relies on these billions of switches working together to make some sensible data. 	
Each unique configuration of switches represents a specific state (e.g: 100010 is a diff state from 010011)

Transistors are these billions of switches present at the core of every computer 

Each activity that we perform on a computer, streaming a video, writing a document, all of this basically boils down to manipulating combinations of 1's and 0's.

Bit (Binary Digit) is the fundamental (most basic level data storage unit) in a computer. A computer group these together to make sensible information, as single bit is too small to represent useable information 

Binary is ideal for electronics as it is simple and stable 
Electrical Efficiency: (2 voltage levels, 0 volts or 5 volts) will always be easier to distinguish than 10 different levels 
Error Resistance: binary signals are less likely to be corrupted 
Simple: circuits are reliable and easier to design 

Hexadecimal is easier to read, has a shorter representation and is used in debugging
